About the Project

For this project we sought to analyze how and why certain types of teams are performing at a high level and how they compare to each other. We began by selecting teams in two different categories. We selected 4 teams that are traditionally dominant and are "big clubs" due to their rich history and financial stature. We then selected 4 teams that are "smaller" and traditionally less dominant teams who are performing at a very high and unexpected level this season. Based on these sub-categories, we selected Real Madrid, Manchester City, Bayern Munich, and Arsenal as the 4 big teams to analyze. For the smaller teams we decided on Stuttgart, Girona, Bayer Leverkusen, and Aston Villa.

The specific motivations for selecting the big teams were straightforward, as they have been at the top of the Soccer world over the last 8 years. The smaller teams on the other hand were a little less straightforward. Terms of success for a small team is relative and can mean a variety of things. In the context of this project we decided to define success in terms of domestic league position. All 4 of the small teams we selected are performing at an unexpectedly high level this season. For example Stuttgart was in a relegation position last year and are now third in the Bundesliga. All of the 'smaller' teams that we selected are overperforming their expected output and thus we have chosen to include them in our analysis.

We wanted to first give context to the differences between the two sub-categories of teams via visualizations which show their performance in the league over time and their financial comparasions overtime. We did this by showing the league position over the last 7 seasons and also comparing their financial situations. The bulk of the data collection process was dedicated to specific player statstics which we gathered from whoscored.com (a large soccer statistics page). We selected players who played at least 70% of games and then compared them with their previous seasons key statistics. The specific statistics we gathered were Goals, Assists, Key passes, and Shots per game. Additionally, for a more holistic analysis we included defensive data. Included in this data was total tackles and interceptions and tackle success rate.
